# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Exact target user

**Primary:** CTO or VP Engineering at a seed-to-Series A startup (5-50 people), who is the de facto budget owner for AI tooling. They're paying for Cursor Business, GitHub Copilot Business, and maybe Claude Team — probably because someone on the team asked for them and they approved without comparing alternatives. They see a $3,000/mo line item on their credit card statement, vaguely feel like it could be lower, but haven't had time to audit it.

**Secondary:** Technical co-founders at pre-seed companies who are personally paying for multiple AI subscriptions and starting to feel the burn as they approach their first fundraise and want to get their costs under control.

**Not the target:** Enterprises with procurement teams, solo developers who already know what they're paying, or non-technical operators who won't understand the recommendations.

## How to get the first 100 users in 30 days with $0 paid budget

**Week 1: Seeding with authentic content**

Post on Hacker News: "Show HN: We audited our AI tool spend and built a free tool to help others do the same." This is not self-promotion — it's a genuine product with a real audit result to share. The key is the honest framing and the free-before-email mechanic. HN respects that.

Post in r/cursor with a specific example: "Used this tool to audit our 8-person team's Cursor spend — found we were on Business when Pro does the same thing for us, saving $160/mo. Built a free tool to do this for any stack." Real numbers, real result. No pitch.

**Week 2: Twitter/X distribution**

Tweet a thread: "I audited what our startup spends on AI tools. Here's what I found:" with real numbers and a link to the audit. The shareable URL mechanic means others can share their own results, creating organic content.

DM 20 CTOs and technical co-founders who have posted about AI tool spending. Not a pitch — "I built this thing, curious what your result would be."

**Week 3: Newsletter drops**

Cold email 5 developer-focused newsletters (The Pragmatic Engineer, TLDR, Bytes, Console.dev) with a "free resource for your readers" angle. These newsletters actively look for free tools to share with their audiences. One placement = 200-500 visits.

**Week 4: Community seeding**

Post in YC alumni Slack, Indie Hackers forums, and Product Hunt Upcoming. These communities over-index on exactly the target user.

## The unfair distribution channel

Credex's existing customer base. Every company that has ever purchased AI credits through Credex is a warm lead who already cares about AI costs. An email to the Credex customer list offering a free audit of their remaining tools would convert at 20-30%+ vs cold traffic at ~2-3%. No paid budget needed, no cold outreach — just a tool sent to people who are already bought in on the problem.

---

## What week-1 traction looks like if this works

- 300-500 site visits from the HN post (typical for a "Show HN" that gains traction)
- 80-120 completed audits (25-30% completion rate from visits to the final results)
- 30-50 email captures (35-40% of completers enter email)
- 8-15 consultation requests (from high-savings cases; ~20% of email captures)
- 3-5 organic tweet/post shares using the shareable URL feature
- 50-80 direct link visits from those shares

**North Star signal:** >25% of people who start the form complete it and view results. Below that, the form is too long or the value proposition isn't clear enough at entry.
